Differential Equations, Bifurcations and Chaos

This is the author website for the book Differential Equations, Bifurcations and Chaos, by Paul C. Matthews, published by < a href="https://www.springer.com>Springer in September 2025 as part of their Springer Undergraduate Mathematics Series.

The book is aimed at undergraduate students in Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry and Engineering. Because of the broad target audience, the book does not include formal mathematical proofs.

Chapter headings

  1. Introduction
  2. Analytical Methods for Differential Equations
  3. Qualitative Methods for First-Order Differential Equations
  4. Second-Order Linear Systems
  5. Second-Order Nonlinear Systems
  6. Bifurcations
  7. Difference Equations
  8. Chaos
  9. Solutions to Odd-Numbered Exercises
  10. Appendix: Essential Background Mathematics

Topics covered include:

  • Separation of variables, integrating factor method
  • Phase lines for first-order equations, stability and instability of fixed points
  • Phase planes for second-order equations, classification of fixed point types
  • Many examples, includin the nonlinear pendulum, dynamics of interacting species, epidemic modelling
  • Systems with parameters and the main bifurcations that occur as these parameters vary
  • Discrete dynamical systems and their similarities and differences compared with continuous ones
  • Chaos in differential equations and in discrete systems
