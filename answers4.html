Answers to Exercises for Chapter 4

Second-Order Linear Systems

  1. As two first-order differential equations, it is $$ \dot x= y, \ \dot y = -4y -5x $$

  2. As a second-order differential equation for \(x\), $$\ddot x =2\dot x -3(6x-y)=\dot x-16x. $$ Similarly for \( y\), $$\ddot y = 6(2x-3y)-\dot y= \dot y -16y,$$ so we get the same second -order equation.

  3. For the system $$\dot x = x + 3 y, \ \dot y = 3x -7y,$$ the matrix form is Then find the specific solution that obeys the initial conditions \(x(0)=5, \ y(0)=0\).

  4. For each of the following matrices, categorise the corresponding linear system by finding the trace and determinant.
    1. $$M=\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{pmatrix}$$
    2. $$M=\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 \\ 3 & 4 \end{pmatrix}$$
    3. $$M=\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 2 \\ -3 & -2 \end{pmatrix}$$
    4. $$M=\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 2 \\ -2 & -1 \end{pmatrix}$$

  5. Find the eigenvalues, and if necessary, the eigenvectors, of these matrices, and describe the behaviour of the linear system for large \( t\).
    1. $$ M = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -3 \\ 4 & -3\end{pmatrix} $$
    2. $$ M = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -3 \\ 4 & -3\end{pmatrix} $$
    3. $$ M = \begin{pmatrix} 2 & -3 \\ 4 & -3\end{pmatrix} $$

  6. Describe the qualitative behaviour of the third-order system $$\dot x = x + y - z, \ \dot y = - y, \ \dot z = 2x+3y -2z . $$

  7. A matrix \(M\) is said to be idempotent if \(M^2=M\). Find an expression for the exponential of an idempotent matrix in terms of \(M\) and \( e \). Hence find the exponential of the matrix $$M=\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 3 \\ 0 & 0\end{pmatrix}.$$
